General history of the world

Posted on 2019-12-30

-虽然古埃及文明早已消亡，美索不达米亚文明(Mesopotamia Civilization)和印度河文明也早已不复存在，但是中国文明却不间断地延续了下来。今天中国可以为拥有世界上最古老持久的文明而自豪。

中国文明不但是最古老的也是最独特的。中国是第一个养蚕并将纤细的蚕丝织成精美丝织品的国家，也是唯一不用动物乳汁和乳制品共人民普遍实用的重要文明。另外，祖先崇拜从远古时代就一直是中国宗教独有的重要特征。对一个人的姓十分重视与这一点有密切的联系。中国人的姓总是位于个人的名字之前，而不像西方那样，位于个人的名字之后。这一习俗反映了再中国社会中起主要传统作用的是家庭，而不是个人，国家或者教会。常见的中国建筑物屋顶华丽，由一排排木柱支承，这种式样也是从远古时代起就已开始的，就像中国人从远古时代就已使用筷子而不使用刀叉一样。

就两性关系而论，中国文明如同其他所有文明，也存在着不平等。性别歧视从婴儿一出生就开始。中国的两性不平等也反映在中国人独特的世界观中；中国人认为世界是阴和阳这两种相互作用、相互补充的元素的产物。阴代表女性、黑暗、弱小、被动；阳代表男性、光明、强大和主动。尽管男性和女生相互补充、缺一不可，但女性天生就是被动的、顺从的。几千年来，男性道德家们从这一哲学命题出发，详尽地阐述了中国妇女如何行事才算完全做到顺从和被动。

-文明的代价之一就是将人分成了统治者和被统治者。

-古典文明时代最明显的特点，就是欧亚大陆趋于整体化。公元一世纪，罗马帝国、安息帝国和汉帝国一起，连成了一条从苏格兰高地到中国海、横贯欧亚大陆的文明地带，从而使各帝国在一定程度上能互相影响。

-基督教和大乘佛教这两大世界性宗教于古典时代后期从各自的发源地中东和印度向外扩张；在随后的几个世纪中，基督教争取到了整个欧洲，佛教赢得了大部分亚洲。这两大宗教取得成功的原因可从他们共同具有的某些特点找到。一个特点是他们都着重灵魂的拯救，允诺来时可以获得永恒的幸福.另一特点是,都坚持平等主义;其团体的大门向所有请求加入的人敞开,不论是男子或妇女,富人或穷人,自由人或奴隶,都一视同仁.最后都强调很高的道德准则,强调要灵魂得救就必须遵守这些准则;这一要求加上有效的教会组织,使这两大宗教能对虔诚徒众的日常生活产生重要影响.

这些特点在古典时代较后几个世纪里特别吸引人.在那几个世纪里,社会动荡,到的混乱,尤其在大城市,情况更是如此。城市里许许多多人感到无家可归，漂泊无定；对这些人，基督教和大乘佛教提供了安慰，保护和知道。当彼拉多问道，真理是什么，表达出时代的绝望情绪时，宗教给予了回答。因而，最早诡异基督教的都是些地位低贱，无所皈依的人，这并非偶然之事。同样，大乘佛教的最大胜利是在中国，在汉朝崩溃之后全国处于混乱阶段，人世间的一些问题似乎找不到解答时取得的。

中国文明

中国文明的特点是聚合和连续。其原因在于中国较与世隔绝，他仅仅收到西北游牧民的侵略。

在中国，与文化统一性一样重要的是，各时期都存在着惊人的政治上的统一。这种同意在很大程度上起因于中国文明——唯一在任何时候都未产生过祭司阶级的文明——的独特的现世主义。固然，皇帝也是祭司，他为了所有臣民的利益而向苍天献祭，但是，他履行的宗教职责，始终居于次要地位。因为，存在于欧亚大陆其他文明中的礁石与俗人之间、教会与国家之间的巨大分裂，在中国是不存在的。中国也没有与印度史诗相当的东西，因为印度史诗饱含玄学，其内容多与个人的灵魂得救有关。中国人的经典都强调人在社会中的生活，尤其是强调家庭成员之间、君臣之间的关系.这种对现世的强烈的偏好为政治组织和政治稳定提供了一个坚固的,根本的基础.而这一基础又由于中国人在这些世纪里实行一种独特的制度–在全国竞争性考试的基础上选任文官–而得到进一步加强.与这类似的情况在西方或其他任何地方过了2000年才出现.

A comment on replication, P-value and evidence by Steven N. Goodnman

Posted on 2019-12-16 | In Reproducibility

Summary

Small P-value usually corresponds to high credibility on the observed alternative hypothesis. However, the replication probability (the probability of repeating a statistically significant result), is substantially lower than expected. Besides, it shows that P-value overstate the evidence against the null hypothesis.

Introduction

In an attempt to establish the credibility of a hypothesis, replication is a main concern. For many researchers, replication means that the repetition of a statistically significant result in a study of adequate power. Besides, the credibility of a result increases as its associated p-value decreases. The connection between replication and the p-value is of big discussion, as it raised a lot of problems. For a give p-value, the probability of replicating a significant result, under certain assumptions, is smaller than many expect. This is the reason why the use of p-value within a hypothesis testing framework, would indirectly into the difficulties in interpreting p-values as a measure of evidence.

Another use of p-value for inference is the measure of the degree of conflict of the data with the null hypothesis. For any observed effect, it is argued that the smaller the associated p-value, the more evidence there is against the null hypothesis, making the hypothesis more likely to be false. In addition, invoking hypothesis test logic, the more likely the null hypothesis is to be false based on one experiment, the more probable that a repetition of that experiment will result in a small p-value and a significant result. It could be shown that the the failure of an experiment to repeat the statistical significance achieved by previous studies often causes concern. A quantitative analysis will show that this concern is based on a fundamental misunderstanding.

To what extent can we trust scientific findings?

Posted on 2019-12-16 | In Reproducibility

Abstract

Concerns about a lack of reproducibility of statistically significant results have recently been raised in many fields. Then the question of how to quantify the reproducibility follows.

Introduction

Scientific discovery centers on empirical testing of research hypotheses. P-value is the standard tool to interpret results in statistical hypothesis testing. How does the standard tool work? A result associated with a P-value below a predefined significance level (typically 0.05) is considered as “statistically significant” and interpreted as evidence in favour of a hypothesis.

The problem with inference based on P-value is that it provides only partial information about the probability of a tested hypothesis being true. As a matter of fact,this probability depends on the statistical power to detect a true positive effect and the prior probability that the hypothesis is true. (e.g. low statistical power increases the probability that a statistically significant effect is false positive, which means that statistically significant results from small studies are more likely to be false positive. Similarly, a lower prior probability for a hypothesis to be true would increase the probability that a statistically significant effect is a false positive). What’s more, this problem is exacerbated by publication bias in favor of speculative findings and against null results.

However, Reproducibility of statistically significant results have recently raised in fields like: medicine, neuroscience, genetics, psychology and economics. The mismatch between the interpretation of statistically significant findings and a lack of reproducibility threatens to undermine the validity of statistical hypothesis testing as it is currently practised in many research fields.

Typically, the judgement of credibility of published results is left to individual researchers.

Example

The prediction markets predict the outcomes of the replications well and work as a promising tool for assessing the reproducibility of published scientific results in the field of psychology.

Redefine statistical significance

Posted on 2019-12-15 | In Reproducibility

The lack of reproducibility of scientific studies has aroused big discussion over the credibility of claims of new discoveries based on ‘statistically significant ‘ findings.

There are a number of possible reasons, like multiple testing, P-hacking, publication bias and under-powered studies. The leading cause, simply, is the low statistical standards of evidence for claiming new discoveries in many field of science. Say, associating statistically significant findings with P < 0.05 results in a high rate of false positives, and further the lack of reproducibility.

Simple step for improving the reproducibility of scientific research is to change the threshold for statistical significance from P < 0.05 to P < 0.005. Thus results currently called significant might not meet new threshold and being called suggestive instead.

There are some restrictions for the proposal for the claims of discovery of new effects. It has no effect on the claims of the replication studies, either confirmatory or contradictory ones. Besides, there is no advocation of change of thresholds in fields that have already adopted more stringent standards (e.g. genomics and high-energy physics research). What’s more, this proposal is for improving reproducibility and other ways of summarizing the data (e.g. Bayes factor) are preferred by many researchers.

In summary, changing the P-value threshold is a simple way to avoid the problem of claims of credibility, and thus the problem of lack of reproducibility with broad acceptance.
